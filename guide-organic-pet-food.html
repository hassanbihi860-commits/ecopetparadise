Guide to Organic Pet Food

Make informed choices about your pet’s diet with organic ingredients.

Organic pet food is made from ingredients grown without synthetic pesticides, hormones or genetically modified organisms. When selecting organic meals for your pet, look for certifications like USDA Organic or equivalent that guarantee at least 95 % of ingredients are organic. This ensures fewer chemical residues and higher nutritional standards.

Transition your pet gradually by mixing increasing amounts of the new food with the old over a week or two. Sudden changes can upset their digestive system. Pay attention to your pet’s energy levels, coat condition and stool quality during the transition. If any issues arise, consult your veterinarian for guidance.

Buying organic supports sustainable farming practices that prioritise soil health, biodiversity and ethical treatment of animals. Although organic food can cost more, it often contains higher‑quality proteins and fewer fillers, which may improve your pet’s long‑term health and reduce vet bills.
