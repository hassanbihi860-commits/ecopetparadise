How to Compost Pet Waste Safely

Manage pet waste responsibly using certified compostable bags.

Composting pet waste reduces landfill contributions and returns nutrients to the soil, but it must be done carefully to avoid spreading parasites and bacteria. Always use bags certified to meet industrial composting standards to ensure they break down properly. Do not use regular plastic or biodegradable bags that may leave microplastics in your compost.

Separate pet waste compost from food scrap compost and never use it on edible plants. Instead, apply it around ornamentals or trees. Maintain a balanced compost pile with carbon‑rich materials like dry leaves and sawdust to reduce odours and improve decomposition. High temperatures (above 60 °C) over several weeks are needed to kill pathogens; a dedicated pet waste composter or hot compost bin can help achieve this.

Monitor your compost regularly and turn it to ensure even breakdown. If you are unsure about reaching sufficient heat, consider using a commercial composting service that accepts pet waste. Following these guidelines keeps your garden safe while managing waste responsibly.
